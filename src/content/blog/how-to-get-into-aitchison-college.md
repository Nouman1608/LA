Every year, thousands of families across Pakistan — and Pakistani families abroad — set their sights on Aitchison College, Lahore. Founded in 1886 and now past its 140th year, it remains the most sought-after boys' school in the country, and its admission process is famously exacting: strict age rules, competitive written papers, an interview, and exactly one attempt per admission cycle.

The good news is that the process is predictable. Families who understand each stage and prepare early give their sons a genuine advantage. This guide walks through every step, based on the most recent admission cycle.

## Step 1: Check the age guidelines first

Before anything else, check your son's date of birth against Aitchison's published age guidelines for his intended entry class. This matters more than most parents realise: **age criteria are enforced rigorously and no waivers are granted**. Boys suspected of being over-age undergo a medical assessment — and if a boy is found over-age for his entry year, an admission offer is withdrawn even after it has been made.

If your son is comfortably within the age band, proceed. If he is borderline, contact the admissions office before paying any fee.

## Step 2: Understand the points of entry

Aitchison's main entry points are in Junior School — **K1 and K2** — with places in higher classes offered when seats fall vacant. There is also a separate admission cycle for **A Level and FSc (H1)** at the senior end. Seat availability by class is published on [aitchison.edu.pk](https://www.aitchison.edu.pk/), and it is worth checking before you apply: if no seats are listed for your son's class, the application fee will not buy him a test.

## Step 3: Gather the documents

The application requires more paperwork than most schools. Based on the last cycle, you will need:

* The completed application form (released on the Aitchison website)
* Three photographs of your son
* His last school report and games record
* Photocopies of both parents' CNICs
* Copies of income tax returns or land revenue challans
* A school declaration form, filled and signed by his current school
* A bank draft for the application fee — in the 2026-27 cycle this was **Rs 55,000 for day scholars and Rs 27,500 for boarding applicants**, payable to Aitchison College, Lahore

## Step 4: Apply before the deadline

Forms are typically released in **September–October**, and the last cycle's deadlines were **30 November for day scholar applications and 31 December for boarding**. Applications go by post or courier to the admissions office, and the college sends an acknowledgement letter once your file is accepted. Aitchison has not yet announced 2027-28 dates, so treat these as a guide and confirm on the official website once forms are out.

## Step 5: Prepare for the written test — early

This is the stage that decides most outcomes. Every applicant sits written papers in **English, Mathematics and Urdu**, pitched at the level of the class he is applying for:

* **English** — comprehension, vocabulary, spelling, sentence construction and grammar. Younger boys (K1/K2) are tested on words and simple sentences.
* **Mathematics** — for K1, pre-number concepts, sets, whole numbers and shapes; for higher classes, numbers, fractions, time, money, graphs and patterns, with real time pressure.
* **Urdu** — comprehension (tafheem) and writing (mazmoon). Boys educated overseas, or in Pakistan for under two years, may qualify for an Urdu exemption, verified against school records.

Remember: **each boy is allowed one appearance per testing cycle**. There is no resit in the same year, which is why families who treat preparation as a months-long project — rather than a final-week sprint — consistently do better. ## Step 6: The test day

A few weeks before the test, a roll number slip arrives by courier with your son's test date and venue. Testing takes place **on campus at Aitchison College** on specified dates for each class. Bring the slip, arrive early, and keep the morning calm — a composed child performs measurably better on timed papers.

## Step 7: The interview

Boys who clear the written papers are interviewed before the Principal makes the final decision. Panels look for confidence, clear spoken expression and general awareness — not rehearsed answers. Mock interviews help enormously here, especially for boys who have never been questioned by unfamiliar adults.

## Step 8: Medical and age verification

As part of selection, the medical team verifies age and general fitness — including height, weight, vision and hearing. This is also where age documentation is scrutinised. There is nothing to prepare here beyond honesty in the application: discrepancies at this stage end candidacies.

## Step 9: The result

Aitchison does not publish a merit list. Families receive an **acceptance or regret letter by courier**, typically within weeks of the final stage. Successful boys join at the start of the new academic session.

## When should preparation start?

Work backwards from the timeline: forms out in September–October, deadlines in November–December, tests on per-class dates after that. A boy starting structured preparation in the summer has months of weekly practice before he sits the paper; a boy starting after the form is submitted has weeks. For the 2027-28 cycle, **now is the right time to start**.
