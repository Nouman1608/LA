Edexcel's International A Level Business (YBS11) spreads the subject across six modular units — three at IAS, three at IA2, with Units 3 and 6 assessing practical business skills through a written exam. The structure lets students build a grade unit by unit, and our course makes each stage purposeful.

The early units establish the fundamentals — markets, marketing, operations, people and finance — while the later units move into strategy, the global environment and data-driven decision-making. The practical-skills papers are handled as their own discipline: interpreting business data, evaluating options and justifying a course of action are skills we rehearse directly, because Edexcel examines them on paper.

Classes are live and small-group, with detailed feedback on the extended, evidence-based answers these units demand — the kind that turn business knowledge into top-band marks.
