Cambridge A Level Physics (9702) runs from everyday mechanics to the strangeness of the quantum world, and tests it across five papers — including a hands-on practical exam and a Paper 5 built around planning, analysis and evaluation. Our teaching is shaped around that complete demand.

We secure the foundations first — measurement, kinematics, forces and energy — before moving through waves and electricity into the fields, capacitance, nuclear and quantum physics that define the A2 material. Experimental method and data analysis are taught as core skills throughout, because under the Cambridge model they are examined in their own right rather than treated as extras.

Classes are live and capped small, with close feedback on both physical reasoning and the precise, well-structured answers these papers reward.
