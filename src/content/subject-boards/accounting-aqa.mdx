AQA's A Level Accounting (7127) is assessed entirely through three written papers, with no modular safety net — which means the whole syllabus, from the first journal entry to advanced variance analysis, has to be exam-ready at the same time. Our course is structured to get students there without gaps.

We start where accounting starts: the concepts and the double-entry system, the trial balance and the adjustments that trip students up at year end. From there the path runs through company and partnership accounts into cost and management accounting — budgeting, variance analysis and the ratio work that turns raw figures into business insight. Throughout, the focus is on accuracy and clear presentation, because AQA's marks reward method as much as the final number.

Lessons are live, capped small, and built around steady past-paper practice so the format holds no surprises on the day.
