Cambridge A Level Economics (9708) asks students to think like economists — building models, weighing trade-offs and applying theory to real markets and economies, from a single firm's pricing to national policy. Our teaching develops that analytical habit from the first diagram onward.

Microeconomics comes first: demand and supply, elasticities, market structures and market failure, with the diagrams and chains of reasoning that anchor strong answers. Macroeconomics follows — national income, aggregate demand and supply, fiscal and monetary policy, trade and exchange rates. Across AS and A2, the papers move from multiple-choice and data response to extended evaluation, so we train precise recall and the balanced, well-judged argument top marks require.

Classes are live and small, with feedback focused on diagram accuracy and the evaluative reasoning examiners reward most.
