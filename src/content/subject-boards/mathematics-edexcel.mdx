Edexcel A Level Mathematics (9MA0) takes the linear route — three papers sat at the end, two in Pure and one combining Statistics and Mechanics — so the whole course has to come together at once. Our teaching builds the connected fluency that approach rewards.

The Pure content runs from proof, algebra and functions through coordinate geometry, sequences, trigonometry and the full sweep of calculus, taking in vectors and numerical methods along the way. Statistics brings in sampling, data presentation, probability distributions and hypothesis testing, while Mechanics covers kinematics, forces and Newton's laws. With everything examined in three final papers, we put real weight on method, accuracy and exam-day problem-solving across all three strands.

Classes are live and small, with each student's working scrutinised line by line — in maths, that is where marks are kept or lost.
