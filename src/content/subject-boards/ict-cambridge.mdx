Cambridge A Level ICT (9626) is a deliberately practical qualification — students do not just learn about information systems, they build and use them, combining theory with genuine hands-on skill in software and data handling. Our teaching keeps that balance front and centre.

The syllabus spans data and information, hardware and networks, software and database management, the systems development lifecycle, and the security, privacy and ethics issues that surround ICT in organisations. Alongside the written theory, the qualification assesses applied skills directly, so we give students real practice with the tools and techniques the practical papers require.

Classes are live and small, blending clear explanation of the theory with guided, hands-on work and individual feedback.
