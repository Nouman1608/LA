Assessed through three linear written papers, AQA A Level Business (7132) asks students to apply business theory to unseen case studies under time pressure — which makes confident, transferable understanding far more useful than memorised definitions. That is what our teaching aims to build.

The course spans business objectives and organisation, marketing, operations, HR and finance, then rises to strategy and decision-making in a changing external environment. Because every paper can draw on any part of the specification and centres on data-rich case material, we practise reading a scenario quickly, picking the relevant tools, and building an evaluated recommendation the examiner can follow.

Lessons are live, capped small, and grounded in current business examples so the theory always has something real to bite on.
