Edexcel's International A Level Physics (YPH11) breaks the subject into six manageable units across IAS and IA2, with Units 3 and 6 assessing practical skills through a written exam — a structure that lets students bank progress as they go. Our course is built to make every unit count.

The IAS units establish mechanics, materials, waves and electricity; the IA2 units move into fields, particle physics and the rest of the advanced content. The two practical-skills papers are taught as a discipline in their own right — designing investigations, handling data and evaluating method — because that is precisely how Edexcel examines them. Throughout, problem-solving and confident use of equations are the through-line.

Classes are live and small, with detailed feedback on the quantitative, multi-step problems that define A Level physics.
