AQA A Level Economics (7136) is assessed through three linear written papers that interleave micro and macro and place real weight on evaluation — the ability to argue both sides and reach a supported judgement. Building that argumentative discipline is the core of our teaching.

Students develop the micro toolkit — markets, elasticities, market structures and market failure — alongside the macro picture of growth, inflation, policy and international trade, then learn to connect the two. Because the papers reward analysis and evaluation over description, we practise constructing chains of reasoning, using diagrams precisely, and weighing policy options against real-world constraints.

Lessons are live, capped small, and built around past-paper essays and data responses, with detailed feedback on structure and judgement.
