Edexcel's International A Level Economics (YEC11) is organised into four themed units — Markets in Action, Macroeconomic Performance and Policy, Business Behaviour, and Developments in the Global Economy — a structure that moves students logically from individual markets out to the world economy. Our teaching follows that progression deliberately.

Unit 1 grounds students in demand, supply, market failure and intervention; Unit 2 turns to macroeconomic performance, aggregate demand and supply, and policy; Unit 3 examines firms, costs, revenues and market structures; and Unit 4 opens onto globalisation, trade, exchange rates and development. Because each unit is examined in its own right, we build both the technical precision and the evaluative argument these papers reward.

Classes are live and small-group, with detailed feedback on the data-response and essay work that defines the qualification.
