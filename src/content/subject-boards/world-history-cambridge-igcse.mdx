Cambridge IGCSE History (0470) brings the twentieth century to life — its wars, revolutions and rivalries — while teaching students to think like historians, weighing causes and evaluating evidence. Our teaching keeps the narrative vivid and the analysis rigorous.

From the causes of the First World War through the rise of authoritarian regimes and the Second World War to the Cold War and decolonisation, the course rewards students who can explain why events unfolded as they did. With Core and Extended tiers shaping the depth required, we focus on structured explanation and the source skills the papers demand.

Sessions are live and small, with feedback that builds both confident knowledge and sharp historical reasoning.
