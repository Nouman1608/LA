AQA A Level Chemistry (7405) takes the linear route: no modular resits, just three written papers sat at the end of the course, the last of which can draw on any part of the specification. That makes long-term retention and synoptic thinking the real challenge — and it is what our teaching is designed to build.

Rather than treating topics as isolated units, we connect them as we go: how bonding underpins energetics, the way redox threads through inorganic and organic chemistry alike, why kinetics and equilibria keep resurfacing in unfamiliar contexts. Physical, inorganic and organic chemistry are interleaved so that, by exam season, a question on transition metals or organic synthesis holds no surprises wherever it appears.

Classes are live, capped small, and heavy on past-paper application — with everything riding on the final sitting, exam stamina and recall matter as much as understanding.
