Cambridge O Level Statistics (4040) is a rarity — a full qualification devoted entirely to handling data, assessed through two structured papers that reward method and accuracy over memorisation. For students who find their footing in numbers, it is one of the most scoring subjects on the timetable, and our teaching plays to that.

The syllabus runs from representing and summarising data into probability, discrete random variables and the normal distribution, before arriving at the techniques that tie it together: sampling and estimation, hypothesis testing, and correlation and regression. We treat each as a skill to be practised until it is automatic, backed by a steady diet of structured questions so that clear, well-reasoned working becomes second nature.

Sessions are live and capped small, so every line of a student's method gets checked — in a subject examined on precision, that is exactly where marks are kept.
