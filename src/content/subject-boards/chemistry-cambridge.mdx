Cambridge A Level Chemistry (9701) asks students to move fluently between three worlds — physical, inorganic and organic — and then prove it across five separate papers, including a dedicated practical exam and a Paper 5 built entirely around planning, analysis and evaluation. It is a demanding structure, and our teaching is shaped around it from day one.

Lessons trace the syllabus in the order that builds confidence: securing atomic structure, bonding and the mole concept before layering on energetics, equilibria and reaction kinetics, then opening up transition-metal and organic chemistry, which dominate the A2 papers. Throughout, we treat data-handling and experimental design as core skills rather than afterthoughts — under the Cambridge model they are examined in their own right.

Classes are live and capped small, so every student gets their working checked, their misconceptions caught early, and the exam-technique drilling that turns understanding into marks.
