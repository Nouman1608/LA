Cambridge O Level Economics (2281) gives students a firm first grounding in how economies and markets work, assessed through two structured-question papers. Our teaching keeps the concepts clear and the answers exam-ready.

The course moves from fundamental ideas like scarcity and choice through demand, supply and elasticities into market structures, market failure and the macroeconomics of national income, policy and trade. We focus on accurate definitions, clear cause-and-effect explanation and the use of simple diagrams, since these are where O Level marks are won.

Lessons are live and capped small, with individual feedback that builds both economic vocabulary and structured-answer technique.
