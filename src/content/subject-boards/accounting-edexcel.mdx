Edexcel's International A Level Accounting (YAC11) is built around two substantial units — The Accounting System and Costing (WAC11), then Corporate and Management Accounting (WAC12) — a structure that moves students cleanly from how accounts are kept to how they are used to run a company. Our teaching follows that arc deliberately.

The first unit grounds students in double-entry principles, control procedures and the financial statements of different organisations, with an early look at costing and even the social and ethical dimensions of accounting. The second pushes into limited companies, investment ratios, cash flow statements, standard costing, marginal versus absorption costing, and project appraisal — the decision-making tools a finance professional actually relies on. We connect the two so that costing never feels divorced from the accounts it draws on.

Classes are live and small-group, with detailed feedback on the extended, structured answers these units demand.
