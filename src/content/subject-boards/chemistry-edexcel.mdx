What sets Edexcel's International A Level Chemistry (YCH11) apart is its modular shape: six units split across IAS and IA2, two of which — Units 3 and 6 — assess practical skills through a written exam rather than coursework. Students can bank marks unit by unit, but each unit rewards precision, and our course is built to deliver exactly that.

We work through the chemistry in step with that structure — grounding the early units in bonding, energetics and redox before pushing into equilibria, transition metals and advanced organic analysis later on. The two practical-skills papers get focused attention of their own: interpreting results, evaluating method and spotting sources of error are rehearsed deliberately, because Edexcel examines them on paper.

Every session is live and small-group, with the individual feedback modular exams reward — fixing a weak unit before it is sat, not after.
