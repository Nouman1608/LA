# Business syllabus change — what teachers need to do differently

**O Level Business Studies 7115 → Business 7081**
**IGCSE Business Studies 0450 → Business 0264**
**Last exams on the old syllabuses: November 2026. First exams on the new: 2027.**

Anyone enrolling from now sits the new syllabus. Anyone sitting in November 2026 stays on the
old one. For roughly the next year we are teaching both.

Cambridge's own change log says only "some content has been removed" and "some topics have
been reorganised". That undersells it. Below is the actual comparison, made by reading both
specifications side by side.

Source: IGCSE 0264 v2 (March 2025) vs 0450 v1. **O Level 7081 was not independently checked** —
Cambridge presents its content as identical to 0264, but that is untested here. Verify before
relying on it for the O Level class.

---

## The single most important change

**Application (AO2) rises from 20% to 30% of the qualification.** Knowledge drops from 40% to 35%.

| Objective | Old | New |
| --- | --- | --- |
| AO1 Knowledge & understanding | 40% | **35%** |
| AO2 Application | 20% | **30%** |
| AO3 Analysis | 25% | **22%** |
| AO4 Evaluation | 15% | **13%** |

On Paper 1, AO1 falls 50% → 40% while AO2 rises 20% → 30%. On Paper 2, AO2 rises 20% → 30%.

**What this means in the classroom:** a student who memorises definitions loses ground. Every
point now has to be tied to the specific business in the stimulus or case study. Also:

- **Paper 1 questions now have four parts, not five** — the mark breakdown has changed.
- **Every Paper 2 part (a) is now an applied question.** There is no longer a "free" recall opener.

Paper structure is otherwise unchanged: two papers, 1hr30 each, 80 marks each, 50/50.

## Content to STOP teaching

This is where time gets wasted. Each of these is in the old syllabus and **absent from the new one** —
confirmed by keyword search returning zero hits across the whole 0264 text.

| Dropped | Was |
| --- | --- |
| **Price elasticity of demand** | 3.3.2 — elastic vs inelastic, significance in pricing. **The largest single drop.** |
| **Gross Domestic Product (GDP)** | 6.1.1 — replaced by plain "economic growth" |
| Government economic objectives (identifying them) | 6.1.2 |
| Needs, wants, scarcity | 1.1.1 |
| Importance of specialisation | 1.1.1 |
| Purpose of business activity | 1.1.1 *(inferred from absence — weaker evidence)* |
| Unincorporated businesses vs limited companies | 1.4.1 |
| Risk, ownership and **limited liability** | 1.4.1 |
| Public corporations / public-sector business organisations | 1.4.1 |
| Objectives of social enterprises | 1.5.1 — social enterprises survive only as a *form* at 1.4.1 |
| Private vs public sector objectives | 1.5.3 |
| Dismissal vs redundancy | 2.3.3 — only "unfair dismissal" and "redundancy" survive, separately |
| Difference between production and productivity | 4.1.1 — replaced by *calculating* labour productivity |
| Difference between profit and cash | 5.3.1 |
| Micro-finance | 5.1.2 — crowdfunding stays |
| Sustainable development (as a concept); global warming | 6.2.1 — sustainability is now a *production practice* at 4.3 |
| Legal controls on location decisions | 4.4.1 |
| Cost-effectiveness of the marketing budget | 3.3.4 |
| How product life cycle stages influence marketing decisions | 3.3.1 — replaced by extension-strategy detail |

**Two skills appear to have been dropped**, inferred from verb changes in otherwise-identical
objectives. Confirm against the specimen papers before dropping them from lessons:

- **Drawing organisation charts** — 0450 said "draw, interpret and understand"; 0264 says *interpret* only.
- **Constructing break-even charts** — 0450 said "construct, complete or amend"; 0264 says *complete or amend* only.

## Content to START teaching

### Genuinely new sub-topics

**4.2 Technology and production of goods and services**
- How technology changes production through automation and mechanisation — e.g. Computer-Aided
  Manufacture (CAM), 3D printing
- How technology improves productivity in the service sector — e.g. contactless payments
- Advantages and disadvantages for businesses **and employees**

**4.3 Sustainable production of goods and services**
- How businesses become more sustainable: renewable energy, fewer resources, less waste, reuse,
  recycling, environmentally friendly products and packaging
- Advantages and disadvantages of becoming more sustainable

**6.5 Pressure groups** — promoted from an example to its own sub-topic: how pressure groups
influence business decisions.

### New material the change log does not mention

Cambridge's change log flags only the two Operations sub-topics and digital communication. In
fact there is a lot more:

- **1.1.1 Factors of production** — land, labour, capital, enterprise. Entirely new.
- **1.3.1 Business plan elements** named — objectives, resources, market research, marketing,
  finance, people, operations (was just "contents of a business plan")
- **1.3.3 Horizontal and vertical integration** named explicitly
- **2.1.1 Recruitment stages** named — job descriptions, person specifications, advertisements,
  agencies, online recruitment; selection via CV, application forms, references, testing,
  assessment centres
- **2.1.2 Employment contract contents** — duties, hours, holiday entitlement, sick pay, and the
  benefits of contracts to both sides
- **2.2.1 Flexible working** — home working, flexible hours
- **2.4.2 Fringe benefits, time-based and piece-rate pay**; praise, employee of the month
- **3.1.2 Calculate market share** — new calculation
- **3.1.4 Segmentation by income and lifestyle**
- **3.2.1 Observation** as a primary research method
- **3.3.1 Extension strategies** — recommend and justify; new markets, new uses, adapting
  product/packaging, increased advertising
- **3.3.2 Dynamic pricing**
- **3.3.3 Agents** as a distribution channel
- **3.3.4 Named sales promotion methods** (vouchers, reward schemes, competitions, discounts) and
  **advertising methods** (social media, targeted email, leaflets, billboards) — each with
  recommend-and-justify
- **4.1.1 Calculate labour productivity**; factors affecting inventory levels
- **4.4.1 Calculate costs** — 0450 only asked students to classify them
- **4.5.1 Recommend and justify** quality control vs quality assurance
- **5.1.2 Named external finance sources** — venture capital, leasing, hire purchase, trade credit
- **5.2.1 Named cash flow features** — inflow, outflow, net cash flow, opening and closing balance
- **5.3.2 Calculations from a statement of profit or loss**
- **5.4.1 Capital employed**; calculate total assets, total liabilities, working capital
- **5.5.3 Limitations of ratio analysis**
- **6.1.2 Effects of tax changes on people's income**
- **6.2.1 Named reasons for globalisation** — transport links, technological change, free trade
  agreements, newly industrialised countries

**Digital communication** (2.3.1) is real but smaller than billed: the methods list now names
virtual meetings, email, text, social media and mobile calls. It is an updated example list
inside an existing objective, not a new sub-topic — 0450 already covered "communication methods
including those based on IT".

## Terminology and reorganisation — update your resources

**Rename throughout:** "income statement" → **"statement of profit or loss"**. Topic 5.3
"Income statements" → **"Profit and loss"**.

**Topic 2 order effectively inverts** — worth rebuilding the scheme of work rather than patching:

| Old | New |
| --- | --- |
| 2.1 Motivating employees | **2.4** Motivating employees |
| 2.2 Organisation and management | 2.2 (+ downsizing, + trade unions) |
| 2.3 Recruitment, selection and training | **2.1 Human resource management** |
| 2.4 Internal and external communication | **2.3 Methods of communication** |

Motivation theory is now merged into one objective: "main motivational theories: Maslow, Taylor,
Herzberg" (0450 separated Maslow from Taylor/Herzberg).

**Other moves:**
- 1.2 "Classification of businesses" → **1.2 Economic sectors**, cut to two bullets
- 1.4 collapsed to a franchise/joint-venture focus
- Old 3.4 Marketing strategy **dissolved** → 3.3.6; foreign markets → 3.3.7; legal controls
  promoted to its own 3.4
- 3.3.5 "Technology and the marketing mix" → **"Ecommerce"**
- Old 4.2/4.3/4.4 shift down to **4.4/4.5/4.6** to make room for the new sub-topics
- Working capital moves out of 5.2 into **5.1.1**, and becomes a calculation at 5.4.1
- Old 6.2 splits into **6.3 Business and the environment** + **6.4 Business and ethical issues**
- **External costs and benefits now sit at 6.2.3, under "Business and the international economy"** —
  counter-intuitive, but that is what the spec says

Topics 1 and 3 are both materially **reduced in size**.

## New formula sheet

0450 had no formula list. 0264 adds one (p23–24). Reproduced in full:

**Marketing** · Market share = (Sales revenue of a business ÷ Total market sales revenue) × 100

**Production** · Labour productivity = Output per period ÷ Number of employees · Total variable
cost = Variable cost per unit × units · Variable cost per unit = Total variable cost ÷ units ·
Total cost = Total fixed costs + total variable costs · Average cost = Total cost ÷ units ·
Break-even output = Fixed costs ÷ Contribution per unit · Contribution per unit = selling price
per unit − variable cost per unit · Margin of safety = Actual sales − break-even sales

**Business finance** · Revenue = Selling price per unit × units sold · Gross profit = Revenue −
cost of sales · Profit = Total revenue − total costs, or Gross profit − expenses · Working
capital = Current assets − current liabilities · Profit margin = (Profit ÷ Revenue) × 100 ·
Gross profit margin = (Gross profit ÷ Revenue) × 100 · ROCE = (Profit ÷ Capital employed) × 100

**Liquidity** · Current ratio = Current assets ÷ Current liabilities · Acid test = (Current
assets − inventory) ÷ Current liabilities — both may be presented as a ratio :1 or as a number

**Newly examinable calculations:** market share, labour productivity, costs (0450 only asked
students to classify), revenue/gross profit/profit from a statement of profit or loss, working
capital. Break-even, contribution, margin of safety and the five ratios were already required —
they are just written down now.

⚠️ **Cambridge changed the Total cost and Margin of safety formulas between v1 (Sept 2024) and
v2 (March 2025).** Any resource built from the v1 spec needs re-checking against those two.

## Open items

1. **O Level 7081 content not independently verified** against 0264. Check before assuming identical.
2. **Statements of financial position** — 0450 said constructing them "will not be assessed". The
   equivalent carve-out was confirmed for statements of profit or loss but **not** for financial
   position. Re-check 0264 5.4.1 before telling students construction is off the table.
3. **The removal list may not be exhaustive.** A handful of tail bullets in 0264 (2.2.1, 3.3.1,
   5.1.2, 5.2.1, 5.4.1, 6.3.1) were not read in full. The high-value terms were swept for, so the
   list above is sound, but a minor removal could hide there.
4. **Specimen papers.** Cambridge says to read the updated specimen assessment materials alongside
   the syllabus. Not reviewed here, and they are the best guide to the new four-part Paper 1
   questions and the applied Paper 2 part (a).
